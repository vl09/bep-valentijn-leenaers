This thesis investigates how to keep document metadata and front matter as a single source of truth shared by MyST and Typst export pipelines. The goal is to let students write their content once while still producing a well-structured thesis PDF with minimal manual formatting. Particular attention is given to the boundary between semantic metadata in MyST and layout-specific decisions in Typst, so that the template remains flexible without becoming confusing to maintain. The resulting workflow aims to be readable, robust for incomplete drafts, and easy to extend with institution-specific front matter when needed.
